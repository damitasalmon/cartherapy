About the Series

Have you ever seen the meme “Parked car conversations are low-key therapy sessions?” Well this is one of the things that inspired this series. I’ve had some of my best and worst conversations in a car, and in talking to people, that seems to be a common thing.

Car Therapy is a 6 episode web series about A down-on-her-luck college therapist who decides to drive for a ride sharing app to make ends meet during a pandemic. On her rides, she is exposed to new people and new parts of LA and always ends up dishing advice, whether solicited or not.

Not only do we need more black stories on screen, we need to have these conversations surrounding mental health. It is slowly breaking out of the taboo category in the black community- and I want to use my series to shed more light on it, in a comedic way. This isn’t intended to throw textbook diagnosis around, but to have real conversations about how each character vents, reacts, or explores a different side of the human experience.

About the Creator

Jennelle Williams is a storyteller at heart, who became a multi-hyphenate (writer/director/producer, and sometimes actor!) to get her stories told. She took the leap to LA after studying film in her hometown of DC, driving across the country for her dreams. Since moving to sunny California, she has worked on several sets, starting out as a PA and working her way up to staff writer on two sitcoms. She plans on taking her own projects to the big screens; writing and developing half-hour shows and feature length films focusing on multi-generational familial, platonic, and romantic relationships from a black woman's point of view.
